                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  <p style=\"MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt\" class=\"MsoNormal\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\"><b style=\"mso-bidi-font-weight: normal\">Chantdiscography.com</b> offers a relational database of Western Plainchant on sound recordings. (The term “record” is used here to describe any sound carrier, including but not limited to shellac 78s, vinyl 45s, vinyl LPs, cassette tapes and compact discs.) It is a revision and expansion of <i>A Gregorian Chant Discography</i>, published in 1990. Data entry began in February 2010 and&nbsp;public access has been offered since November 2010. <font color=\"#000000\"><font><span style=\"font-style: normal\"><span style=\"font-weight: normal\">The
entry of known records issued since 1990 was the first priority, followed by the entry of records listed in the </span></span></font></font><font color=\"#000000\"><font><span style=\"font-style: normal\"><span style=\"font-weight: normal\">1990
book. Both are&nbsp;</span></span></font></font></font><span style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\">complete, and new issues and records newly
discovered are still being entered.</span></p><p style=\"MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt\" class=\"MsoNormal\"><br></p><p style=\"MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt\" class=\"MsoNormal\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\"><font color=\"#000000\"><font><span style=\"font-style: normal\"><span style=\"font-weight: normal\">Each&nbsp;</span></span></font></font></font><span style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\">chant is identified by one or more editions. The first page number identifies the edition heard on the record. The following&nbsp;page numbers identify editions that may be the same editions or different variant versions of the same chant. </span><font style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\" color=\"#000000\">For
example, most hymns found in LH, AM and LU vary in text or melody or
both; the doxology, or final strophe, is usually a different text in
each of the three editions. A chant sung from none of the editions
listed is noted as a variant manuscript or variant edition with the
source cited if known. Chants that are</font><font style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\" color=\"#000000\">&nbsp;not found in any modern liturgical edition may be identified from one
of various sources identified in the list of abbreviations. Some chants have not been found in any available source.<br></font></p>
