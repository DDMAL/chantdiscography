                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  <font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\"><strong>Liber Usualis</strong> 
</font><p></p>
<p>
	
	
	


</p><p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">The <i>Liber Usualis</i> is a
collection of chants for parish use. It was published in several
editions, among them 1895 (Dom Pothier\'s first edition), 1903 (Dom Mocquereau\'s revision), 1912 (based on the Vatican Editions of the <i>Graduale Romanum </i>and the<i> Antiphonale Romanum</i>) and 1924 (</font><span style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\">Desclée</span><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">&nbsp;No. 780), the pagination changing as
new chants were added. The publisher, Desclée, changed their serial
number each time. It included most of the <i>Graduale Romanum</i>,
the principal exception being the weekdays of Lent. It included
Vespers for Sundays and feast days from the <i>Antiphonale Romanum</i>,
and Matins for eight principal Offices, the only modern edition of
Matins. It also had miscellaneous chants for various services such as
Benediction. The 1895 edition was based on the 1895 edition of <i>Liber Gradualis</i>, edited by Dom Joseph Pothier. The 1903 edition was largely re-edited by Dom André</font><font size=\"3\"><span style=\"font-family: arial;\">&nbsp;Mocquereau; it was the</span></font><span style=\"font-family: arial; font-size: medium;\"> basis for most of the 1904 recordings. Under the Vatican Commission chaired by Dom Pothier, the <i>Kyriale</i> of 1905, the <i>Graduale Romanum</i> of 1908 and the <i>Antiphonale Romanum</i> of 1912 reverted to Dom Pothier\'s earlier readings, with some of Dom Mocquereau\'s readings adopted for the Mass chants.&nbsp;</span></p>
	
	
	



<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	The 1934 edition, Desclée No. 801,
was the last newly paginated edition. Pages were added to subsequent
editions without greatly disturbing the basic pagination. But even
the 1934 printing had one addition. Pages 1357 and 1357 were replaced
by pages 1357, 1357v, 1358 and 1358v to include the hymn <i>Festivis
resonent</i>.</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	The 1938 printing had two more additions.
Pages 1351, 1352, 1353 and 1354 were replaced by pages 1351, 1351v,
1352, 1352v, 1353, 1353v, 1354 and 1354v for the chants of the Mass
of the feast of St. John Bosco. Pages 1633 and 1634 were replaced by
pages 1633, 1633v, 1634 and 1634v for the Mass of the Feast of the
Seven Dolours of the B.V.M. The 1938 printing is the basis for the LU
numeration in this discography. Any page numbers that are found in
any later printing are indexed as LV (revised), but there is no
particular printing that contains all of these.</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	After this printing, page numbers were
added not with the “verso” terminology but with superscript
numbers. The 1947 edition had four additions. Page 262 became page
262<sup>12</sup> and pages 262<sup>1</sup> to 262<sup>11</sup> were
added to provide the Common Commemorations of Saints for Vespers. The
content of page 1123 was changed and pages 1122<sup>1</sup>, 1122<sup>2</sup>,
1122<sup>3</sup>, 1122<sup>4</sup>, 1122<sup>5</sup> and 1122<sup>6</sup>
were added for the Common of One or Several Sovereign Pontiffs. Page
1612 was replaced by the new feast of the Immaculate Heart of Mary
added on pages 1612, 1612<sup>1</sup>, 1612<sup>2</sup>, 1612<sup>3</sup>
and 1612<sup>4</sup>. The feast of St. John Leonard was added to
pages 1681<sup>1</sup>, 1681<sup>2</sup>, 1681<sup>3</sup> and 1681<sup>4</sup>.</font></p><p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">The 1950 edition has one notable change: <i>O filii et filiae</i> (LU 1875) is printed uniquely in modern notation with metrical rhythm.</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	In the 1952 edition pages 1600 to 1606
were replaced by new chants for the feast of the Assumption of the
Blessed Virgin Mary, including pages 1600<sup>1</sup>, 1600<sup>2</sup>,
1600<sup>3</sup> and 1600<sup>4</sup> added in sequence. 
</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	After this printing, page numbers were
added not with the “verso” terminology or superscript numbers but
by adding capital letters. The 1956 edition had many changes. Pages
Lj to Lxcvij were added to the introduction as “Changes in the Liber
Usualis” to summarize the ritual changes of March 23, 1955. Credo V
and VI were moved into place following the first four Credos,
changing all the page numbers from 73 to 94 without changing the
contents. (Both LU and LV page numbers are cited for all of these
chants.) Pages 279A to 279V were added to provide antiphons and
psalms for the Little Hours of feasts below First Class. In the most
drastic change of pagination, the new order of Holy Week (decreed in
November 1955) changed the contents of pages 578 to 777 to new
content on pages 578 to 776 plus 776A to 776Z and 776AA to 776MM.
(Both LU and LV pages are cited in the discography with “LU – ”
or “LV – ” for chants not found in one or the other.) Page 952
was changed and pages 952A and 952B were added for the hymn <i>Sacris
solemniis</i>. The votive Mass of the Christ the Eternal High Priest
changed the content of page 1283 and added pages 1283A to 1283D. The
feast of Ss. Philip and James was moved from May 1 to May 11; the
page numbers from 1448 to 1452 became 1465A to 1465F, and page 1447
was numbered “1447-1451”. The feast of the Blessed Virgin Mary
the Queen (May 31) was added on pages 1476A to 1476H. Chants for the
feast of Ss. Gervase and Protase were added to page 1492 with the
addition of pages 1492A and 1492B. Chants for the feast of St.
Paulinus were added to page 1495 with the addition of pages 1495A and
1495B. Chants for the feast of the Finding of the Body of St. Stephen
the First Martyr were added on pages 1583A and 1583B (this is hard to understand, since the feast was dropped from the universal calendar in 1955; but see page Lxxxvi). The feast of
St. Pius X was added on pages 1623A to 1623F, moving the antiphon
<i>Gloriosae virginis</i> from LU 1623 to LV 1623F. Pages 1675 and
1676 gained additional chants on pages 1675, 1676, 1676A and 1676B.
The chant <i>Auctor</i> was added to page 1876, the chant <i>Caelitum</i>
was added to page 1879, and page 1880 became 1882, followed by a new
Appendix II on pages 1* to 68*.</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	The 1961 printing (as well as 1962 and
1963) had a few changes. Pages Li to cix replaced the 1955 additions
to the introduction as “Changes in the Liber Usualis” to
summarize the ritual changes of 25 July 1960. St. Joseph the Workman
(May 1) replaced the Solemnity of St. Joseph (end of April). For this
new content, pages 1437 to “1449-1451” replaced pages 1437 to
1447 (which was pages 1437 to “1447-1451” after the removal of
Ss. Philip and James in 1956). In the Supplement for certain
religious orders, the feast of the Eucharistic Heart of Jesus (pages
1-10 after the Blessed Virgin Mary, Mediatrix of All Graces) was
omitted. In the Supplement for the United States, three antiphons for
First Vespers were omitted (<i>Fulcite me, Labores manuum, Candor
est</i>), changing the pagination and reducing it by two pages.</font></p>
<p style=\"margin-bottom: 0in\"><font face=\"arial\" size=\"3\">	This account is based on examination
of some copies of LU. Please advise the compiler of any errors,
oversights or omissions.</font></p>
